Functional Materials Discovery Using Machine Learning.

September 2021


Brief

The project was aimed at discovering new perovskite materials for renewable energy application. Perovskites are semiconductor materials efficient in the conversion of solar energy. Some properties of perovskite materials have earned perovskites attention in the the quest by scientists in the discovery of highly functional and low cost materials for solar energy harvesting, and storage.

The project involved detail data analysis in feature engineering and algorithm selection. The data were sourced from the Materials Project repository. Also, Machine learning algorithms were used to accurately produce materials which displayed the desired properties; the property which classes a material a metal, semiconductor, or a non-metal.

The Support Vector Regression (SVR), the K-Nearest Neightbor (KNN), and the Random Forest Regression (RF) algorithms were used in the prediction of the perovskite materials. The RF algorithm best performed with an R2 value of 0.842. However, the RF algorithm has been proven in studies as a better estimator or predictor of the class of perovskite material studied (the ABO3 group). Thus, affirming the accuracy of feature selection made for this project.
