Effects of Acid Rain on Mechanical Properties of Perewinkle Shell Ash (PSA) Blended Cement Mortar.

November 2016


Brief

In this project aimed at studying the effects of simulated acid rain on some mechanical Properties of PSA mortar blended with cement at several proportion/ratio. These mechanical properties include the compressive strength, flexural strength, and the loss in mass of the materials.

The project was to tackle the issues caused by the dilapidation of structures in areas of high gas flaring and fossil fuel environmental pollution. Acid rain is an effect of high industrialised activities releasing gasses such as carbon dioxide, nitrogen oxide, and sulphur dioxide into the atmosphere. These gases form in the atmosphere and are released to the lithosphere as acid rain.

This rain is known to affect structural materials, flora, and faunas in areas of which they occur. The study examined the extent to which some locally sourced binding structural materials (PSA) interacted with rain of several acidity levels occuring in gas flaring communities.
